
# Service Manual - 2007 Royal Enfield Machismo 500 Chrome

## Introduction
This service manual provides **detailed maintenance procedures, troubleshooting steps, and repair guidelines** for the **2007 Royal Enfield Machismo 500 Chrome**. Proper servicing ensures longevity, reliability, and optimal performance.

---
## 1. Technical Specifications
### Engine
- **Type**: 499cc, single-cylinder, 4-stroke, air-cooled
- **Power**: 22 HP @ 5500 RPM
- **Torque**: 40.85 Nm @ 3000 RPM
- **Compression Ratio**: 8.5:1
- **Ignition**: CDI Unit
- **Fuel System**: Carburetor
- **Valve Clearance**:
  - **Intake**: 0.08mm (cold)
  - **Exhaust**: 0.20mm (cold)

### Transmission
- **Type**: 5-speed, constant mesh
- **Clutch**: Wet, multi-plate
- **Primary Drive**: Chain

### Brakes & Suspension
- **Front Brake**: Disc, 280mm
- **Rear Brake**: Drum, 152mm
- **Front Suspension**: Telescopic fork
- **Rear Suspension**: Twin shock absorbers

### Electrical System
- **Battery**: 12V, 14Ah
- **Charging Output**: 13.5V - 14.5V @ 3000 RPM
- **Spark Plug**: NGK BPR5ES (0.7 - 0.8mm gap)

## 3. Maintenance Procedures

### A. Engine Oil Change
#### Tools Required:
- 17mm socket wrench
- Drain pan
- Funnel
- 15W-50 API SL grade oil (~2.25L)

#### Steps:
1. **Warm up** the engine for 5 minutes.
2. Place a **drain pan** under the engine.
3. Remove the **drain bolt** and let oil drain completely.
4. Replace the **oil filter**.
5. Reinstall the drain bolt and tighten to **20Nm torque**.
6. Pour **new oil** and check the level.
7. Start engine, run for **1 minute**, then recheck oil level.

### B. Valve Clearance Adjustment
#### Tools Required:
- Feeler gauge (0.08mm, 0.20mm)
- 10mm wrench
- Flathead screwdriver

#### Steps:
1. **Remove cylinder head cover**.
2. Rotate engine to **TDC (Top Dead Center)** on compression stroke.
3. Use feeler gauge to check clearance:
   - Intake: 0.08mm
   - Exhaust: 0.20mm
4. Adjust using **locknut & adjuster screw**.
5. Reassemble and test engine.

### C. Carburetor Tuning
#### Steps:
1. Warm up engine.
2. Adjust **idle speed** screw (1100 Â± 100 RPM).
3. Adjust **air-fuel mixture** for optimal performance.
4. Sync carburetor if necessary.

### D. Chain Adjustment
#### Steps:
1. Place the motorcycle on **center stand**.
2. Loosen rear axle nut and **adjust chain tension**.
3. Maintain **20-25mm slack**.
4. Tighten axle nut and recheck alignment.
